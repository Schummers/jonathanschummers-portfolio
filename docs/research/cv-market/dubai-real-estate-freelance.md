# Designing for Dubai's wealth machines: family offices, luxury developers, and DAMAC

A senior Product Designer with UX research, product management chops, and real-estate domain knowledge sits at an unusually rare intersection in the Gulf. **Dubai hosts roughly 75% of the Middle East's family offices and the world's largest concentration of branded luxury residences**, yet most of these organizations run their real-estate operations on Excel and ageing CRMs. The structural opportunity is real: family offices are publicly under-invested in operational tech (72% admit so per Deloitte 2024), luxury developers are mid-way through Salesforce-led transformations, and **DAMAC's $25–30 billion data-centre pivot plus its 50,000-home customer base have created exactly the kind of greenfield product surface where a fractional senior designer can move the needle**. This report maps the terrain in two parts: the structural and role landscape across family offices and luxury developers (Area 1), and a deep DAMAC-specific dossier (Area 2) ending with concrete pitch angles.

# AREA 1 — Family offices and luxury developers in Dubai/GCC

## How GCC family offices are actually organized

Dubai is home to roughly 75% of the Middle East's ~290 single-family offices, with ~30% of MENA family-office investable assets booked in the city (Knight Frank / Invest in Dubai). Two regulatory shifts matter for a vendor: DIFC's **Family Arrangements Regulations 2024** raised the SFO threshold to **US$50 million net assets** (from $10m) and removed DFSA licensing for SFOs; ADGM, DMCC and DWTC offer parallel SFO regimes. The market has bifurcated cleanly into pure SFOs and operating-conglomerate-attached offices, and the design and tech buying patterns differ between them.

A sizable Dubai SFO ($500m+ AUM) typically runs nine functions — Investment Management, Real Estate, Private Equity / Direct Investments, Philanthropy, Family Governance, Tax & Legal, Operations / Finance, IT / Digital, and Concierge / Lifestyle — but the **median Middle East family office employs only ~9 staff, of whom three are family members in leadership roles** (Knight Frank/Campden). Crucially, **75% of Middle East family-office CEOs are themselves family members**, far higher than Western norms — meaning sales cycles are relationship-driven, not procurement-driven. Operating-conglomerate-attached SFOs (Al-Futtaim, Dubai Holding, Al Habtoor, DAMAC Capital) are a different beast: hundreds of professionals embedded inside the group's tech function, with named CTOs (Himanshu Shrivastava at Al-Futtaim; Oliver Skagerlind as Group CTO at Dubai Holding).

Real-estate portfolios are managed via three overlapping vectors: **direct freehold** (most common, often dating from ruler-granted land), **JVs through a developer subsidiary** (Al Habtoor Real Estate, Al-Futtaim Real Estate, BurJuman by Al Ghurair), and increasingly **proprietary fund vehicles** alongside Brookfield, Starwood, KKR. The pain points are remarkably consistent across families: **Excel-heavy rent rolls, fragmented data across properties spanning Dubai-London-Cairo-Beirut, no unified dashboard linking NOI to family wealth, and stale book valuations**. Yardi Voyager, MRI Software, and Argus Enterprise dominate where any system exists; Eton Solutions opened a Dubai office in September 2023 and partnered with Asas Capital in July 2025 to launch the region's first "Family Office as a Service" — a clear signal that vendor-led modernization is the active wedge.

### Hiring posture and the freelance gap

The data here is unambiguous: **43% of family offices globally are rolling out a tech strategy this year, while 72% admit being under-invested in operational tech** (Deloitte 2024). And yet pure SFOs typically have **zero to one** dedicated digital staff member. Hiring is almost entirely referral-driven, mediated by boutique consultancies — Frontier Path (Dubai), LOGIC Consulting, Cavenwell, Alpha FMC, IQ-EQ, Apex, Ocorian — and by platform vendors (Eton, Yardi, Aleta) who increasingly need local UX and implementation talent. Toptal explicitly markets a "freelance family-office consultant" pool. The single highest-leverage networking move is the **Prestel & Partner Family Office Forum Dubai** combined with GPFO membership.

### Key Dubai/GCC family offices with real-estate exposure

The relevant targets, ranked by digital openness and real-estate concentration, fall into three tiers. **Operating-conglomerate-attached offices** (most procurement-mature): Al-Futtaim Group (Omar Al Futtaim; flagship Dubai Festival City; CTO Himanshu Shrivastava; 350+ executives in AI training), Al Habtoor Group (Al Habtoor City on the Dubai Water Canal, Polo Resort, plus Budapest and Lebanon assets), Al Ghurair Group (BurJuman, Reef Mall, Al Ghurair Centre — and **Sultan Al Ghurair's October 2025 Bloomberg-announced new luxury developer** is a fresh greenfield SFO real-estate vehicle), and Dubai Holding (the Maktoum personal portfolio, ~$75bn assets, now consolidating Nakheel + Meraas + Dubai Properties under MD Khalid Al Malik).

**Younger-generation-led vehicles** (most receptive to product/UX vendors): Abbas Sajwani's AHS Properties (DAMAC family branch — the $120m "Big Ben" Dubai purchase 2025), Bin Ghatti (CEO Muhammad BinGhatti, branded JVs with Bugatti/Mercedes-Benz/Jacob & Co), and Crescent Enterprises' CE-Ventures (Jafar family, Sharjah/Dubai, $83bn AUM).

**Established trading-house SFOs** with significant but conservative real-estate footprints: Saeed & Mohammed Al Naboodah Holding (built Palm Jumeirah and Expo 2020 site), S.S. Lootah Group (Dubai Islamic Bank founders), Olayan Group (Saudi/Liechtenstein, ORECO real-estate vehicle), Kanoo Group (~$20bn AUM Bahrain/UAE), AW Rostamani, Juma Al Majid, Al Tayer, Al Fahim, and the ultra-discreet Al Qasimi Family Office ($15bn AUM, Blackstone/Coller/ICG LP).

Gargash Group is a confirmed UAE Huwala-origin conglomerate founded in 1918 and run today as four verticals — automotive (sole Mercedes-Benz agent in Dubai and the Northern Emirates since 1958), financial services (Daman Investments, Deem Finance), F&B, and real estate (Gargash Real Estate develops residential, commercial and industrial properties; Buildan Development was created in 2008 as a real-estate VC vehicle). Worth verifying directly before any pitch is personalized.

## Luxury developers — where the digital roles actually live

The dominant pattern post-2022 is a **holding company with vertically separated CEO-led entities**. Aldar is the textbook case: Group CEO Talal Al Dhiyebi sits over **Jonathan Emery (Aldar Development), Jassem Busaibe (Aldar Investment), and Khaled Alrajhi (Aldar Estates — running Khidmah and Provis)**; the group has a **CDO (Harry Nakichbandi per RocketReach — verify on LinkedIn before outreach)**, an AVP Innovation (Maan Al Awlaqi who signed the Hub71 PropTech MoU), and a CDO-grade leader at each operating company. Emaar centralizes its digital function inside **Emaar Technologies** (a separate subsidiary that builds EmaarOne, MyEmaar, and the Salesforce-powered broker registration platform), led at group level by **CDO Veresh Sita** (ex-CIO of Alaska Airlines and Colliers International) under CEO Amit Jain and founder/MD Mohamed Alabbar. Sobha Realty is run by founder PNC Menon and MD Francis Alfred with **CIO Guruswamy P.** (ex-Dubai Holding) plus Group Head Process & IT Fahad Deshmukh — explicitly **IT-led, not product-led**. Dubai Holding Real Estate (post-2024 Nakheel + Meraas + Dubai Properties merger) sits under **Group CTO Oliver Skagerlind** with Khalid Al Malik leading real estate. Mid-tier (Omniyat, Bin Ghatti, Ellington, Select, MAG, Arada) typically has no dedicated CDO and runs on outsourced or 1–3-person digital teams.

### The product surface that actually exists

The customer-facing portfolio is more mature than family offices but more fragmented than tech buyers expect. **Emaar's EmaarOne** (v6.2.97 in March 2026, with an "AI One Mind" feature, voice service requests, digital handover, online payment) and **MyEmaar** (broker app, Salesforce-powered) sit alongside hospitality (myportal.emaar.com), community portals, and 360 VR tours. **Aldar's Live Aldar** is a single super-app spanning portfolio view, online payments, Digital NOC for resale, AMC, education fees, and UAE Pass login; Aldar Experts handles brokers; **Darna** is the loyalty program (170k+ downloads, ENBD co-branded credit card, cross-portfolio rewards). Sobha runs a basic owner portal plus Sobha Connect for agents on a Microsoft Dynamics 365 F&O backbone. Nakheel and Meraas each have their own apps in the consolidation phase. **CRM is overwhelmingly Salesforce** (Emaar customized by 4C Dubai since 2018; Omniyat on Salesforce; Aldar mixed Salesforce/Oracle); Sobha is the Microsoft Dynamics outlier.

### Engagement model and rates

The hybrid model dominates: developers run lean in-house product teams plus heavy reliance on **Publicis Sapient (early SapientRazorfish client at Emaar), Accenture Song / Fjord Dubai, EPAM Middle East, 4C Dubai, Tigerspike (now Concentrix Catalyst), Robusta, and 360Bright Media** for VR/AR sales-center work. Toptal has a confirmed track record placing senior PMs into named developer engagements — Tony Pagliocco worked on Aldar's digital twin and a "Fast Track sales platform"; Basak Akcam Trotter rebuilt a UAE developer's buyer journey, taking NPS from -17 to +35. Salary bands for a senior Product Designer cluster between **AED 22k–38k/month base** at developers (top quartile up to AED 42k) with freelance day rates of **AED 1,500–3,000 typical, up to 3,500 for niche luxury or PropTech mandates**. Senior PM roles run AED 30–50k/month full-time. Contract scope is typically 3–6 months extendable.

### The PropTech ecosystem layer

Dubai's PropTech aggregation is concentrating fast. Property Finder (Permira/Blackstone-led raise of $525m in 2025) invested in Stake in November 2025 and acquired Mortgage Finder, becoming a de-facto aggregator. Huspy (Balderton-backed, $101m+ raised) acquired Home Matters in 2022. The **Dubai PropTech Hub launched July 2025 with founding partners Binghatti, Majid Al Futtaim, Sobha, Union Properties, and Transguard**, targeting 200+ startups by 2030, while DIFC + DLD's PropTech 2033 white paper pegs the opportunity at **AED 53bn per year**. Hub71 in Abu Dhabi runs equity SAFEs up to AED 750k for PropTech startups; DIFC FinTech Hive runs the Dubai program. The signal: **2026–2027 will see a step-up in digital-product budgets at developers**.

### Where luxury actually differentiates digitally

Dubai is the world's #1 market for **branded residences** — 132 projects, 43,000+ units, 51 completed and 48 in pipeline (Savills 2024) — commanding a 42–86% premium. Beyond DAMAC's Cavalli/de GRISOGONO/Versace stable, Emaar runs Armani Residences in Burj Khalifa, Meraas runs Bulgari Resort & Residences and the Bulgari Lighthouse, Bin Ghatti has Bugatti/Mercedes-Benz/Jacob & Co, Arada has Armani Beach Residences with Tadao Ando, Select has Six Senses Marina, Khamas runs Ritz-Carlton Residences, Enshaa runs Palazzo Versace, Oriental Pearls has Tonino Lamborghini, and Taraf has Karl Lagerfeld Villas. **Each branded residence requires its own luxury digital storytelling** — micro-sites, concierge integrations, AR/VR tours — and the design quality across these projects is uneven, which is itself an opportunity.

## How a Product Designer/PM profile maps to these organizations

Synthesizing across both segments, the strongest positioning is **not** "I do UX." It is a **dual-axis pitch**: real-estate domain fluency × product/SaaS thinking. Three angles dominate.

The first angle, "**Design the internal tools they need to manage assets**," works best in family offices and conservative developer back-offices. Concrete deliverables include real-estate portfolio dashboard MVPs (NOI, capex, valuation, leasing pipeline, mortgage debt) integrated to Yardi/MRI; investor/family-member portals at consumer-grade UX; document-management and KYC onboarding flows; and AI-overlay UX for document parsing, capital calls, and transaction tagging (working alongside Arch, Canoe, Asseta, EtonAI). Vendor-selection and RFP support — Yardi vs MRI vs Eton vs Addepar — sits at exactly the intersection of UX, real estate, and PM.

The second angle, "**Lead digital transformation of buyer/investor experience**," targets developers with established Salesforce stacks. Buyer-journey redesign, broker portal UX (MyEmaar / Aldar Experts / Sobha Connect / DAMAC 360), handover and snagging apps, owner/community/loyalty product strategy, and Salesforce-Lightning / Experience Cloud customization are all live workstreams with named owners. Sub-contracting to Publicis Sapient or Accenture Song is the institutional path; direct LinkedIn outreach to CDOs is the relationship path.

The third angle, "**Build the product function from scratch,**" maps to younger-generation vehicles and PropTech scale-ups. Abbas Sajwani's AHS Properties, Sultan Al Ghurair's new luxury developer, Bin Ghatti, Arada, PRYPCO, Stake, Keyper, and Silkhaus all fit. The pitch is fractional Head of Product: hiring framework, roadmap, design system, research practice — built on a 3–6-month engagement.

The most relevant **target job titles** in order of fit are: Senior Product Designer, Lead Product Designer, Senior Product Manager (Real Estate / PropTech), Product Owner, Digital Product Strategist, Head of Digital Experience, Digital Transformation Manager, and (for family offices) Chief of Staff or Head of Innovation. The **CV signals that resonate** are: named real-estate clients or projects (Yardi/MRI experience is a quiet flex), Salesforce or Dynamics 365 product fluency, measurable outcomes (NPS deltas, conversion lifts, snag-resolution time reductions), branded-residence or luxury-hospitality work, GCC presence and DET/DIFC/DMCC freelance permit, and any prior contract experience with developer accounts via Toptal or Big-4 advisory.

# AREA 2 — DAMAC Properties deep dive

## How DAMAC became a $36 billion sales engine

Hussain Sajwani founded the **DAMAC Group in 1982** with a Dubai catering business that today serves 150,000 meals daily as Global Logistics Services. The property arm — **DAMAC Properties — was incorporated 28 January 2002** when Dubai opened freehold ownership to foreign buyers. The first delivery was Marina Terrace in 2006 (203 units). Sajwani survived the 2008 collapse, then anchored the brand to AKOYA (2013) and the Trump International Golf Club Dubai partnership the same year. **December 2013** brought the LSE GDR listing at $12.25 raising $379m at a $2.65bn valuation, followed by a January 2015 DFM listing. After ~$427m in COVID-era losses, **Sajwani's BVI vehicle Maple Invest Co. took DAMAC private at AED 1.30/share for ~$595m, completing 7 March 2022**.

The privatization unlocked an extraordinary growth phase. **2024 revenue ran ~AED 8.8bn (+69% YoY) with net profit ~AED 4.2bn (+180%)** based on bond disclosures aggregated by Moody's; the **sales backlog hit AED 67bn (~$18.3bn)** with ~$5bn cash on hand. **2025 sales reached AED 36 billion**, ranking #1 private developer in the UAE/Middle East — driven by the **DAMAC Islands launch (December 2024) which set a Guinness World Record at AED 10.2bn in <24 hours**, and **DAMAC Islands 2 (November 2025) at AED 11bn in 5 hours**. Moody's upgraded the company to Ba1 stable, citing the pre-completion-customer-payments funding model (no project debt). DAMAC has now delivered **~50,000 homes** with another 54,000 under construction, employs ~6,800 staff across 6 continents, and operates in 15 global cities (UAE, Saudi Arabia, Qatar, Jordan, Lebanon, Iraq, Oman, Maldives, Canada, US, UK).

### The umbrella, the family, the leadership

DAMAC Properties is **100% owned by Hussain Sajwani via Maple Invest Co. Ltd. (BVI)**. Sajwani's net worth is **~$10.2 billion** (Forbes 2025). The DAMAC Group umbrella spans nine entities: **DAMAC Properties** (Dubai master developer), **DAMAC International** (overseas master communities), **DAMAC Hotels & Resorts / DAMAC Maison** (8 properties), **DAMAC Digital** (data centres, formerly Edgnex), **DICO Investments** (the Sajwani family office, co-investing with Vista, Silver Lake, a16z, Thoma Bravo, KKR, Brookfield, Warburg Pincus), **Vision Investments / Auriel** (owns Roberto Cavalli acquired 2019, de GRISOGONO acquired 2022), **Mirage Leisure & Development**, **DAMAC Capital Partners**, and the original **Global Logistics Services**.

The third generation runs the operating businesses: **Ali Sajwani** as Managing Director, Operations & Technology (and crucially, **the de-facto digital transformation owner — running CRM, MIS, handovers, IT, marketing/communications, and hospitality**); **Amira Sajwani** as Managing Director, Sales & Development and founder/CEO of PRYPCO; **Abbas Sajwani** as Group board member and CEO of AHS Properties; and Mehdi Sajwani in a quieter role. Notable C-suite supporting them include **Sandip Bhatt (CIO, DICO)**, **MP John (CHCO)**, **Hitesh Dhoot (CFO, DAMAC Properties since 2008)**, **Rahul Tewari (CFO, DAMAC Digital, June 2025)**, **Aqil Ali (Vice Chairman, DAMAC Digital)**, **Mohammed Tahaineh (GM Projects)**, **Niall McLoughlin (SVP Communications, International)**, and **Majid Al Ghazali (SVP Projects International / MD DICO Iraq)**. **No public CTO or CDO title exists** — all technology reports up to Ali Sajwani. This is the single most important fact for a freelance Product Designer's pitch.

### The business model and brand stack

DAMAC monetizes through **off-plan sales as the core engine** (50/50 to 75/25 payment plans funded entirely by customer pre-payments, no project debt), supplemented by **branded-residence pricing premium of 20–30%**, hospitality, rentals, asset management, the new data-centre arm, private equity returns through DICO, luxury IP licensing through owned brands, and the DAMAC Mall (1.3m visitors annually). The **brand partnership stack** is the deepest in the GCC: Trump Organization (Trump International Golf Club Dubai 2017, AKOYA villas, recently announced Trump Tower Abu Dhabi rights December 2024), Roberto Cavalli (owned — Cavalli Tower, Cavalli Couture at $6m+ per unit, Cavalli Estates, DAMAC Bay), de GRISOGONO (owned — Safa One, Safa Two 84-floor, Canal Heights, Chic Tower), Versace Home (Damac Tower Nine Elms London, £200m Lendlease build), Fendi Casa, Paramount Hotels (DAMAC Towers by Paramount, $1.35bn sales), Bugatti (Bugatti Residences Business Bay), Mandarin Oriental (Bolidhuffaru Reef Maldives, opening 2025), Radisson, Rotana, Tiger Woods Design, Zuhair Murad, and **Chelsea FC** — front-of-shirt sponsor since May 2025 with Chelsea Residences in Dubai Maritime City.

Five strategic vectors are running in parallel. **The $20 billion EDGNEX/DAMAC Digital US data-centre pledge** announced January 7, 2025 at Mar-a-Lago is the headline bet — 2,000 MW across 8 states over 4 years, "potentially doubled," with the first land closing in Canton, Ohio in December 2025 ($36.5m). **DAMAC Digital now operates in 12 countries** with 25+ data centres and ~4 GW planned capacity; its sub-platform deals include $2.3bn / 144 MW in Jakarta, €400m / 40 MW in Madrid, €150m JV with Greece's PPC, $100m JV with Vodafone Turkey, $1bn with ProEN Thailand, and the April 2025 Hyperco acquisition for the Nordics. **The Saudi Arabia push** includes a likely Riyadh regional HQ and existing Damac Towers Riyadh / Al Jawharah Jeddah plus 35 MW of data-centre campuses. **The Miami Delmore project** (Zaha Hadid-designed, 37 ultra-luxury units on the former Champlain Towers South site, $35–150m+ per unit) launched sales January 2025 but has reportedly **closed zero units as of April 2026**, with DAMAC seeking a JV partner. **The Iraq market entry** with DAMAC Hills Baghdad (October 2025) opens a new geography. # Conclusion: where the leverage actually sits

The most non-obvious finding is that **DAMAC's lack of a public CTO/CDO is not a gap — it is the pitch**. Tech runs through Ali Sajwani as a function inside Operations, which means a fractional senior Product Designer can be hired against a defined SOW without navigating an entrenched product organization. The same principle applies one tier up: **family offices have shifted in 2025 from "tactical digitization" to "operational confidence"** (the Simple 2025 Family Office Software Report's exact framing), and operating-conglomerate-attached SFOs (Al-Futtaim, Dubai Holding, DAMAC Capital) have established procurement for contractor product work.
