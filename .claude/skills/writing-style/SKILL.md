# Writing Style — Jonathan Schummers

## When this skill applies

Any English text a recruiter, hiring manager, or buyer will read: CV bullets,
summaries, taglines, portfolio copy, cover letters, outreach.

Does NOT apply to: code, internal docs, brainstorm notes, casual chat, or
French-language outreach (separate skill).

## Workflow: draft, audit, final

1. **Draft.** Write following the rules. Do not edit while writing.
2. **Audit.** Scan against the red flags below. Rewrite anything that trips one.
3. **Final.** Read out loud. If it sounds like a consultant or a LinkedIn
   ghostwriter, rewrite. If it sounds like Jonathan, ship.

## Audit checklist (AI-writing red flags)

### Content
1. **Significance inflation.** "pivotal moment", "stands as testament", "marking a turning point". Cut or replace with a fact.
2. **Notability name-dropping.** Brand names without context. Replace with one specific anecdote.
3. **-ing analyses with no substance.** "showcasing", "highlighting", "reflecting", "underscoring". Remove or expand with a real source.
4. **Promotional language.** "nestled", "vibrant", "boasts", "renowned", "in the heart of". Replace with a fact.

### Language
5. **AI vocabulary.** delve, realm, intricate, pivotal, showcase, testament, tapestry, underscore, garner, foster, interplay, enduring.
6. **Copula avoidance.** "serves as", "stands as", "features", "represents". Use is or has.
7. **Negative parallelisms.** "It is not just X, it is Y". State the point directly.
8. **Rule of three.** Forced triplets ("innovation, inspiration, and insights"). Use the natural number of items.
9. **False ranges.** "from startups to enterprises", "from X to Y". List directly.
10. **Passive voice / subjectless fragments.** "Was responsible for X". Name the actor.

### Style
11. **Em dashes (—).** Never. Replace with period, line break, comma, or colon.
12. **Boldface overuse.** Bold only the 3-5 keywords that drive the scan.
13. **Inline-header lists.** "**Performance:** Performance improved". Convert to prose.
14. **Title Case Headings.** Use sentence case.
15. **Emojis.** None in CV, cover letter, or business email.
16. **Hyphenated word pairs.** "cross-functional, results-oriented". Drop the hyphens or rewrite as a verb.

### Communication
17. **Chatbot artifacts.** "I hope this helps", "Let me know if you'd like". Remove.
18. **Filler phrases.** "In order to" to "To". "Due to the fact that" to "Because".
19. **Excessive hedging.** "could potentially possibly". Use may.
20. **Generic conclusions.** "The future looks bright". Replace with a specific fact.
21. **Persuasive authority tropes.** "At its core". "It is important to note that". State the point directly.
22. **Signposting announcements.** "Let's dive in". Start with the content.

## Core rules

### Voice
First person. Specific over general. Every claim tied to an impact (finish the
sentence "...which means that..."). Strategic humility. No filler.

### Banned vocabulary (hard cut, never use)
spearheaded, leverage, robust, cutting-edge, world-class, seamlessly,
synergies, game-changer, disruptive, passionate, proven track record, delve,
realm, intricate, pivotal, showcase, showcasing, testament, tapestry,
underscore, foster, garner, nestled, boasts, renowned, in the heart of.

### Bullet formula
`Action verb + Scope + Tool or method + Metric`. No metric, no bullet.
Hard cap ~200 characters per bullet (≈ 2 lines). If it runs longer, split or cut.

### Longer narratives
Structure the thought with STAR in one flow: state the Situation and Task
concisely, then spend the words on the Actions you led and the Result. The
impact lives in Action and Result, not in the setup.

Portfolio case studies have their own workflow: see the `case-study` skill.

## Output format

Hand the text back ready to paste. No preamble, no "Here is your text:", no
explanation unless asked. If the rewrite was substantial, append one short note
(1-2 bullets) listing key changes.
