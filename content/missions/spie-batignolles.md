### Headline (v0)

Harmonizing construction industry processes through the design of a digital activity report

### Subtitle (v0)

Rethinking Spie Batignolles' activity report using design thinking to redefine each module and harmonize business processes

### Context & Objectives (v0)

- Spie Batignolles took advantage of the implementation of a new ERP system to reorganize and harmonize its processes across various modules, including Personnel and Equipment Tracking, Procurement, and Project Monitoring.
- Our mission was to understand and challenge the current processes for each module of the activity report, then design and iterate on a new digital solution.
- The goal of the activity report was to simplify their previous tools, improve performance, and ease data entry, which was often manual and very time-consuming.

### Challenges (v0)

The real challenge of this project was precisely to help the client redefine these processes through design thinking. The client wanted to avoid a top-down approach to process harmonization, favoring an approach based on the needs of the field and the users.

### Target & Method (v0)

Our team gathered the needs of the operational staff for each module of the project through interviews, stakeholder mapping, and "as-is" user journeys.

During workshops, we used projective scenarios and mockups to co-create the solution with our users, ensuring that the activity report reflected the reality of the field and their needs.

- 12 user interviews
- 16 workshops
- 4 stakeholder mappings
- 4 user journeys
- 12 user tests

### Product Team (v0)

- We did not work with an Agile framework on this project.
- 1 Lead UX Designer & 2 Junior UX/UI Designers (including myself)
- About ten people responsible for the ERP implementation.

### Impact (v0)

- Streamlined procurement workflows, reducing request processing time through centralised processes
- Reduced manual data entry by implementing auto-fill functionality
- Redesigned user-centric workflows gathered from on-site construction crews

### Personal Development (v0)

- First long-term assignment with a client.
- I gained immense learning through mentoring from the Lead UX Designer.
- Notable progress in UI/Visual design thanks to my role as an executor and the large number of screens I worked on.
- I am very pleased with my progress as a facilitator: at the beginning, I was afraid of making mistakes and hesitant to speak up during workshops with clients. By the end of the assignment, I took leadership on certain less critical topics.

### Headline

Designed an intuitive SaaS that bridges construction field workers to a complex Microsoft Dynamics ERP, replacing 3 legacy tools with one simple interface for personnel, equipment and procurement

### Subtitle

Designed Spie Batignolles' new digital activity report alongside an ERP rollout, using design thinking and 16 bottom-up co-design workshops to harmonise processes across personnel, equipment tracking, procurement and project monitoring, without imposing a top-down framework on the field crews who would otherwise have bypassed it.

### Context

**âïļ Problem:** Spie Batignolles was rolling out a new ERP and used it to harmonise its activity report across 4 modules (Personnel Tracking, Equipment Tracking, Procurement, Project Monitoring). The business goals were clear: cut manual data entry, speed up procurement, give site managers real-time monitoring. The real challenge was not the software. It was adoption. A top-down redesign would be bypassed by construction crews who have their own way of working.

**ðŊ Target audience:** Construction site managers and foremen who log their crews' hours, equipment use and material orders every day.

**ðŊ Team:** 1 Lead UX/UI Designer (+10 yrs) + Me (UX/UI Designer), inside a ~10-person ERP implementation team.

**ð Key results:**

- 4 modules harmonised in one interface adapted to operational site workflows, with all data centralised in a single ERP, replacing 3 legacy tools
- 12 site users rated the tool positively during usability tests on data-entry ease and navigation logic, with an average SUS score of 83

### How I solved this problem?

1. **Mapped the on-site ecosystem to identify the stakeholders and the two key users who fill the report every day: site managers and foremen**

   ![Stakeholder map of the on-site ecosystem](/images/Experiences/SPIE Bat/spie-bat-research-stakeholder-map.webp)

   We started by mapping the on-site ecosystem with the team. The goal: identify who is actually involved, how they are organised, which interactions matter, and whose validation we would need at each phase. For Spie, this surfaced our two key users (site managers and foremen), the roles to invite to workshops, and the decision-makers to keep in the loop on the side.

2. **Ran 12 interviews with site managers and foremen, then mapped the pain points and opportunities they raised into as-is user journeys per module**

   ![As-is user journey synthesis matrix](/images/Experiences/SPIE Bat/spie-bat-research-activity-matrix.webp)

   We ran 12 on-site interviews with site managers and foremen, then translated what they raised into as-is user journeys, module by module. Each journey gave us a visual base to align stakeholders on what to fix first, and a shared reference every later workshop returned to.

3. **Ran 4 alignment workshops with stakeholders and business reps to prioritize the problems worth solving: cut manual entry, accelerate procurement, and remove the filling errors that broke downstream monitoring**

   We ran 4 alignment workshops with stakeholders and business representatives from each module: real users with 10+ years on site. Using a simple prioritization matrix, we decided which pain points were worth solving in v1 of the new tool and which would wait. Top of the list: cutting time spent on manual data entry, accelerating procurement requests, and removing the recurring filling errors that broke downstream project monitoring.

4. **Co-designed the new business and user workflows in 12 workshops with site crews, business owners and the ERP Lead Tech in the same room**

   ![To-be user flow for procurement](/images/Experiences/SPIE Bat/spie-bat-research-procurement-to-be-flow.webp)

   We led 12 co-design workshops with site crews, business owners and the ERP Lead Tech in the same room. The key was preparation: each session opened with the as-is journeys and a draft of to-be user flows, so the conversation started on a concrete artefact rather than opinions. Those visuals let non-digital participants join the discussion fast, and gave the technical team something tangible to align on too. Design's contribution was less the screens themselves than the materialisation of solutions, which made the group converge faster.

5. **Designed the new SaaS connecting the 4 modules, with automated entry, smart pre-filled dropdowns and group entry for crew pointage to cut the most repeated manual tasks**

   ![Resource time tracking screen, web view](/images/Experiences/SPIE Bat/spie-bat-app-resource-time-tracking.webp)

   We designed the new SaaS that connects the 4 modules into a single source of truth on each construction site, turning project monitoring real-time instead of catch-up. Most of the screen work targeted the most repeated daily entry tasks: automated entry from context, smart pre-filled dropdowns to remove repetitive picks, and group entry to point several crew members at once. Each pattern compounded across every site and every shift, which is where the time savings on manual data entry actually came from.

6. **Positive feedback from site crews during 12 user tests (average SUS score of 83), with strong iteration on versioning and historisation**

   We ran 12 usability tests with site crews and applied a System Usability Scale (SUS) survey at each round. The 12 participants gave positive feedback on data-entry ease and navigation logic, with an average SUS score of 83. The biggest design reshape came on versioning and historisation after these tests: the team needed to see who changed what on a report and when, which v1 had not fully captured.
